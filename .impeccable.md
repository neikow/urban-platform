## Design Context

### Users
Citizens of the Marseille region engaging with urban planning decisions; content editors and administrators managing publications, events, and voting. Used primarily during daytime hours on desktop and mobile. The emotional register is civic engagement — people who want their voice to count in real decisions about their city.

### Brand Personality
Civic, grounded, forward. The platform represents a real place — Mediterranean, sun-warmed, historically layered — not a tech product. Trust comes from clarity and confidence, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone:** Civic & trustworthy — authoritative without being bureaucratic, approachable without being folksy
- **Avoid ALL of:** Generic SaaS aesthetics, government portal clichés, startup energy, NGO charity look
- **Theme:** Light — daytime civic use context
- **Display font:** Big Shoulders Display (condensed, architectural, bold — evokes city signage and civic infrastructure)
- **Body font:** Jost (humanist geometric grotesque — warm, readable, not a design-school cliché)
- **Primary color:** orange (oklch 0.637 0.194 31.558) — unchanged, anchors the palette
- **Secondary:** evolved to deep Mediterranean navy (oklch 0.37 0.13 256) — replaces teal
- **Accent:** warm limestone/sand (oklch 0.84 0.07 78) — replaces magenta
- **Neutrals:** tinted warm toward primary orange hue (replacing the cold blue-gray)
- **Layout:** Asymmetric, editorial. Typography does the work. Not card grids.
- **Button shape:** Architectural (small rounded corners, not pill-shaped)

### Design Principles
1. **Place before product** — every decision should evoke Marseille and Mediterranean civic life, not generic "platform" aesthetics
2. **Typography does the work** — strong, condensed display type carries the visual weight; color is structural not decorative
3. **Civic confidence** — layouts feel deliberate and authoritative, never tentative or template-like
4. **Warm not cold** — palette, spacing, and weight should feel warm and human, not clinical
5. **Earn the scroll** — each section must have a clear reason to exist and reveal something worth seeing
